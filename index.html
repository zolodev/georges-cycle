What is this?

This is an interactive visualization of the 18-year real estate and economic cycle first described by Henry George (1839–1897) in his landmark work Progress and Poverty (1879), and later popularized by Fred Harrison and others.

The original card — "Periods When to Make Money" — was compiled in 1872, copyrighted 1883, and shows three rows (A, B, C) of repeating cycle years spanning from 1780 to 2059.

The Three Rows Explained

  • A Panic Years — Years in which panics have occurred and will occur again. These mark the top of the cycle, where credit collapses, real estate crashes, and financial crises peak. Approximately every 18–20 years.
  • B Good Times / Sell — "Years of Good Times, High prices and the time to sell stocks and values of all kinds." These are the boom years of the cycle — high land prices, credit expansion, euphoria. The right time to reduce exposure.
  • C Hard Times / Buy — "Years of Hard Times, Low Prices and a Good time to buy stocks, 'Corner lots', goods, etc. And hold till the 'boom' reaches the years of good times, then unload." These are the trough years — the best buying opportunities.

How the Cycle Works

The cycle is driven primarily by land speculation. As economies grow, land values rise faster than wages or productive output. Speculators borrow against land, prices become untethered from reality, credit tightens, and a crash follows — roughly every 18 years.

Historical panic years that match the model: 1819, 1837, 1857, 1873, 1893, 1907, 1929, 1945*, 1973, 1989, 2008 (* modified by WWII). The next predicted peak zone: ~2026–2028, with a recession trough around 2033–2035.

How to Use This Tool

  • The chart always opens centered on the current year, showing ±10 years by default.
  • Use the −1 / +1 / −5 / +5 buttons to scroll the center year backward or forward in time.
  • Use the Range ± slider to zoom out (show more years) or zoom in (show fewer).
  • Hover or tap any point on the chart to see details about that year and its phase.
  • The golden vertical line always marks the current real-world year.
  • Press Reset to return to the current year, centered.

The Zigzag / Tooth Pattern

The distinctive triangular "tooth" pattern in the original card represents the oscillation between the three cycle phases. Each tooth corresponds to roughly one full cycle (~18 years). The peaks of Row B (good times) align with the nadirs of Row A (panics), and the valleys of Row C (buy opportunities) fall between.

The numbers between the teeth (8, 9, 10 etc.) represent the approximate number of years between each named event year.
